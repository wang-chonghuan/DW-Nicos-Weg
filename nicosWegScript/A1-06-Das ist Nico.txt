**LISA:**
**Nico!**  
**Translation:** Nico!  
**Explanation:**  
- Simply calling Nico’s name.

**NICO:**
**Stopp!**  
**Translation:** Stop!  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Stopp"** is a direct command to halt, similar to "stop" in English.

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Sag mal, spinnst du? Was ist denn?**  
**Translation:** Tell me, are you crazy? What is it then?  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Sag mal"** means "Tell me" or "Say".
  - **"Sag"** is the imperative form of "sagen" (to say).
  - **"mal" adds a conversational tone, often used for emphasis.
- **"spinnst du?"** means "Are you crazy?"
  - **"spinnst"** is from "spinnen" (to be crazy).
  - **"du"** means "you".
- **"Was ist denn?"** means "What is it then?"
  - **"Was"** means "what".
  - **"ist"** means "is".
  - **"denn"** adds emphasis or curiosity.

**NICO:**
**Die Tasche!**  
**Translation:** The bag!  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Die Tasche"** means "the bag".

**EMMA:**
**Oh, oh …**  
**Translation:** Oh, oh …  
**Explanation:**  
- An expression of concern or realization.

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Die Tasche? Welche Tasche?**  
**Translation:** The bag? Which bag?  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Welche Tasche?"** means "Which bag?"
  - **"Welche"** is the feminine form of "which".

**LISA:**
**Warte, Emma!**  
**Translation:** Wait, Emma!  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Warte"** is the imperative form of "warten" (to wait).

**NICO:**
**Die … die Tasche in there!**  
**Translation:** The ... the bag in there!  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Die ... die Tasche in there!"** is a mix of German and English, indicating where the bag is.

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Da ist keine Tasche.**  
**Translation:** There is no bag.  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Da"** means "there".
- **"ist keine Tasche"** means "is no bag".
  - **"keine"** is the feminine form of "no" or "none".

**LISA:**
**Hallo, ich bin Lisa Brunner. Das ist Nico. Nico sucht seine Tasche.**  
**Translation:** Hello, I am Lisa Brunner. This is Nico. Nico is looking for his bag.  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Ich bin Lisa Brunner"** means "I am Lisa Brunner".
- **"Das ist Nico"** means "This is Nico".
- **"sucht seine Tasche"** means "is looking for his bag".
  - **"seine"** is the possessive form for "his".

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Und?**  
**Translation:** And?  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Und?"** is a prompt for more information.

**LISA:**
**Ist Nicos Tasche in Ihrem Taxi?**  
**Translation:** Is Nico’s bag in your taxi?  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Ist Nicos Tasche"** means "Is Nico's bag".
- **"in Ihrem Taxi"** means "in your taxi?"
  - **"Ihrem"** is the dative form of "your" (formal).

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Nicos Tasche? Nein. Die Tasche ist nicht im Taxi.**  
**Translation:** Nico’s bag? No. The bag is not in the taxi.  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Die Tasche ist nicht im Taxi"** means "The bag is not in the taxi".
  - **"im"** is a contraction of "in dem" (in the).

**NICO:**
**Nein. Wo?**  
**Translation:** No. Where?  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Wo?"** means "Where?"

**TAXIFAHRER:**
**Keine Ahnung, wo deine Tasche ist. Hier ist die Tasche nicht.**  
**Translation:** No idea where your bag is. The bag is not here.  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Keine Ahnung"** means "No idea".
  - **"Ahnung"** means "idea" or "clue".
- **"wo deine Tasche ist"** means "where your bag is".
  - **"deine"** is the possessive form for "your".
- **"Hier ist die Tasche nicht"** means "The bag is not here".

**LISA:**
**Komm, wir suchen die Tasche! Suchen. Komm, Nico!**  
**Translation:** Come, we will look for the bag! Search. Come, Nico!  
**Explanation:**  
- **"Komm"** means "Come" (imperative form).
- **"wir suchen die Tasche"** means "we will look for the bag".
  - **"suchen"** means "to look for" or "to search".
- **"Suchen"** is a repetition for emphasis.
